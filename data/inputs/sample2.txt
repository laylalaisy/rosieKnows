RAG Evaluation Overview

Evaluation is an important component of a Retrieval-Augmented Generation system. It helps measure how well the system retrieves relevant documents and generates correct answers.

There are two main aspects of evaluation:

1. Retrieval Quality
This measures whether the correct chunks are retrieved for a given query. Metrics may include recall, precision, and ranking quality.

2. Answer Quality
This measures whether the generated answer is correct, grounded, and complete.

Common problems in RAG systems include hallucination, irrelevant retrieval, and missing context.

To improve evaluation, developers often create a benchmark dataset with known questions and expected answers.
